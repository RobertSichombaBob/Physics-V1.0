ECON 110: Principles of Economics

Advanced Microeconomic and Macroeconomic Theory

Overview Syllabus Lectures Research Resources

Course Overview

This PhD-level course provides a rigorous foundation in economic theory with emphasis on mathematical formalization, empirical testing, and contemporary research applications. Students will develop advanced analytical skills and contribute to ongoing economic research.

The course covers advanced microeconomic theory including consumer and producer behavior, general equilibrium, game theory, and information economics. Macroeconomic components focus on dynamic stochastic general equilibrium models, economic growth, and monetary theory.

Advanced Econometrics
Time series analysis, panel data, and causal inference methods
Game Theory
Strategic interactions, Nash equilibrium, and mechanism design
General Equilibrium
Walrasian equilibrium, welfare theorems, and market efficiency
Behavioral Economics
Bounded rationality, prospect theory, and experimental methods

Mathematical Foundations

Optimization Theory

\( \max_{x \in X} f(x) \text{ subject to } g(x) \leq 0 \)

Constrained optimization using Kuhn-Tucker conditions and Lagrange multipliers for economic applications.

  • Kuhn-Tucker necessary conditions
  • Envelope theorem applications
  • Dynamic optimization problems

General Equilibrium

\( \sum_{i=1}^n x_i(p) = \sum_{i=1}^n \omega_i \)

Existence, uniqueness, and stability of competitive equilibria in Arrow-Debreu economies.

  • Walras' law and excess demand
  • Brouwer fixed point theorem
  • Welfare theorems

PhD Level Advanced

MATH 110: Business Mathematical Methods

Advanced Mathematical Methods for Economic Analysis

Overview Syllabus Lectures Proofs Resources

Course Overview

This PhD-level course provides rigorous mathematical foundations for economic analysis, focusing on optimization theory, dynamic systems, stochastic processes, and computational methods. Students will master advanced mathematical techniques essential for economic research.

The course emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical implementation, with extensive use of Python, R, and MATLAB for solving complex economic problems. Students will develop proofs, implement algorithms, and apply methods to real economic data.

Optimization Theory
Convex analysis, Kuhn-Tucker, and dynamic programming
Dynamic Systems
Difference equations, stability analysis, and phase diagrams
Stochastic Processes
Markov chains, Brownian motion, and stochastic calculus
Computational Methods
Numerical optimization, Monte Carlo, and finite element methods

Mathematical Foundations

Dynamic Programming

\( V(x) = \max_{u \in \Gamma(x)} \{ F(x,u) + \beta V(T(x,u)) \} \)

Bellman equation and value function iteration for solving dynamic economic problems.

  • Contraction mapping theorem
  • Policy function iteration
  • Euler equation methods

Stochastic Processes

\( dX_t = \mu(X_t)dt + \sigma(X_t)dW_t \)

Stochastic differential equations and It's lemma for modeling economic uncertainty.

  • Martingale theory
  • It isometry
  • Feynman-Kac formula

Proof Techniques

  • Fixed point theorems and applications
  • Measure theory and integration
  • Functional analysis in economics
  • Asymptotic analysis and limits
