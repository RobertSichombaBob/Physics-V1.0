Problem 1: Unit Conversions (20 points)

20 pts

Convert the following quantities to SI units (meters, kilograms, seconds):

(a) 15.6 km to meters
(b) 45 mph to m/s
(c) 2.3 × 10⁴ g to kg
(d) 1.5 days to seconds

Problem 2: Scientific Notation & Prefixes (20 points)

20 pts

Express the following using appropriate SI prefixes:

(a) 3.2 × 10⁹ watts
(b) 4.7 × 10⁻⁶ meters
(c) 8.9 × 10³ grams
(d) 5.6 × 10⁻¹² seconds

Also, write the following prefix expressions in scientific notation:

(e) 15.3 MW
(f) 0.45 ns

Problem 3: Dimensional Analysis (20 points)

20 pts

Using dimensional analysis, determine if the following equations are dimensionally consistent. Show your reasoning.

(a) v² = v₀² + 2as

where v and v₀ are velocities, a is acceleration, and s is distance

(b) T = 2π√(L/g)

where T is time, L is length, and g is acceleration

(c) F = mv²/r

where F is force, m is mass, v is velocity, and r is distance

Problem 4: Vector Operations (20 points)

20 pts

Given two vectors: A = 3i + 4j and B = -2i + 6j

(a) Calculate the magnitude of vector A
(b) Calculate the dot product A · B
(c) Calculate the cross product A × B (assume 2D vectors in xy-plane)
(d) Find the angle between vectors A and B

Problem 5: Physical Applications (20 points)

20 pts

A particle moves in a circular path of radius 5 meters with a constant speed of 10 m/s.

(a) Using dimensional analysis, show that the centripetal acceleration a is proportional to v²/r, where v is speed and r is radius.

(b) Calculate the centripetal acceleration of the particle.

(c) If the particle has a mass of 2 kg, calculate the centripetal force required to maintain this motion.

(d) Express your answer from part (c) in both newtons and kilonewtons.
