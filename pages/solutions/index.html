Problem 1: Unit Conversions - Solutions

12 pts

A carpet is to be installed in a room whose length is measured to be 13.71 m and whose width is measured to be 4.46 m. Find the area of the room.

Key Concept

Area of a rectangle = length × width. Pay attention to significant figures in the final answer.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Identify the given values:

Length = 13.71 m, Width = 4.46 m

2 Apply the formula for area of a rectangle:

Area = Length × Width = 13.71 m × 4.46 m

3 Perform the multiplication:

13.71 × 4.46 = 61.1466 m²

4 Apply significant figures rules:

- Length has 4 significant figures (13.71)

- Width has 3 significant figures (4.46)

- The result should have 3 significant figures (same as the measurement with fewer significant figures)

5 Round to appropriate significant figures:

61.1466 m² → 61.1 m² (3 significant figures)

Final Answer

Area = 61.1 m²

Note

When multiplying measurements, the result should have the same number of significant figures as the measurement with the fewest significant figures. In this case, the width (4.46) has 3 significant figures, so our final answer should also have 3 significant figures.

Problem 2: Volume Calculation - Solutions

12 pts

An object in the shape of rectangular parallelepiped measures 2.0 in × 3.5 in × 6.5 in. Determine the volume of the object in m³.

Key Concept

Volume = length × width × height. Convert inches to meters using 1 in = 0.0254 m.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Identify the given values:

Length = 2.0 in, Width = 3.5 in, Height = 6.5 in

2 Calculate volume in cubic inches:

Volume = 2.0 in × 3.5 in × 6.5 in = 45.5 in³

3 Convert inches to meters:

1 in = 0.0254 m, so 1 in³ = (0.0254 m)³ = 0.0254³ m³

4 Calculate the conversion factor:

0.0254³ = 0.0254 × 0.0254 × 0.0254 = 1.6387064 × 10⁻⁵ m³/in³

5 Convert volume to cubic meters:

Volume = 45.5 in³ × (1.6387064 × 10⁻⁵ m³/in³) = 7.45611412 × 10⁻⁴ m³

6 Apply significant figures rules:

- All measurements have 2 significant figures

- The result should have 2 significant figures

7 Round to appropriate significant figures:

7.45611412 × 10⁻⁴ m³ → 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ m³ (2 significant figures)

Final Answer

Volume = 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ m³

Note

When converting between units, pay attention to cubic conversions. 1 cubic inch equals (0.0254 m)³, not 0.0254 m³. All original measurements have 2 significant figures, so the final answer should also have 2 significant figures.

Problem 3: Significant Figures - Solutions

12 pts

A rectangular plate has a length of (21.3 ± 0.2) cm and a width of (9.8 ± 0.1) cm. Calculate the area of the plate including its uncertainty.

Key Concept

For multiplication, the relative uncertainty is the sum of the relative uncertainties of the factors.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Identify the given values:

Length = 21.3 ± 0.2 cm, Width = 9.8 ± 0.1 cm

2 Calculate the nominal area:

A = 21.3 cm × 9.8 cm = 208.74 cm²

3 Calculate relative uncertainties:

Relative uncertainty in length = 0.2 / 21.3 = 0.00939 (0.939%)
Relative uncertainty in width = 0.1 / 9.8 = 0.01020 (1.020%)

4 Calculate relative uncertainty in area:

Relative uncertainty in area = 0.00939 + 0.01020 = 0.01959 (1.959%)

5 Calculate absolute uncertainty in area:

Absolute uncertainty = 208.74 cm² × 0.01959 = 4.087 cm²

6 Round uncertainty to appropriate significant figures:

- The uncertainties (0.2 and 0.1) have 1 significant figure

- The absolute uncertainty should be rounded to 1 significant figure

4.087 cm² → 4 cm² (1 significant figure)

7 Round area to match uncertainty:

208.74 cm² → 209 cm² (rounded to match uncertainty of 4 cm²)

Final Answer

Area = 209 ± 4 cm²

Note

For multiplication, the relative uncertainty of the product is the sum of the relative uncertainties of the factors. The absolute uncertainty should be rounded to 1 significant figure, and the measured value should be rounded to the same decimal place as the uncertainty.

Problem 4: SI Prefixes - Solutions

12 pts

Express the following using appropriate SI prefixes:

Key Concept

SI prefixes represent powers of 10. Common prefixes: milli (10⁻³), micro (10⁻⁶), nano (10⁻⁹), pico (10⁻¹²), kilo (10³), mega (10⁶), giga (10⁹).

Step-by-Step Solution

1 (a) 0.00085 L

0.00085 L = 8.5 × 10⁻⁴ L = 0.85 mL (milliliters)

2 (b) 5.44 × 10⁻¹¹ g

5.44 × 10⁻¹¹ g = 54.4 × 10⁻¹² g = 54.4 pg (picograms)

3 (c) 73,000,000 m

73,000,000 m = 7.3 × 10⁷ m = 73 Mm (megameters)

4 (d) 9.450 s

This value is already in a convenient form with appropriate significant figures. No prefix needed.

9.450 s (no prefix needed)

Final Answers

(a) 0.85 mL
(b) 54.4 pg
(c) 73 Mm
(d) 9.450 s

Note

When using SI prefixes, choose the prefix that gives a numerical value between 1 and 1000 when possible. For example, 73,000,000 m is better expressed as 73 Mm rather than 0.073 Gm or 73,000 km.

Problem 5: Dimensional Analysis - Solutions

13 pts

Analyze the following equations to determine whether dimensionally correct or not:

Key Concept

For an equation to be dimensionally correct, all terms must have the same dimensions. Trigonometric functions are dimensionless.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 (a) v = u + ax

Dimensions:

[v] = [u] = L/T (velocity)
[a] = L/T² (acceleration)
[x] = L (distance)
[ax] = (L/T²) × L = L²/T²

The equation adds L/T (velocity) and L²/T² (acceleration × distance), which have different dimensions.

L/T ≠ L²/T² → Dimensionally INCONSISTENT

2 (b) y = (2m)cos(kx) where k = 2m⁻¹

Dimensions:

[y] = L (length)
[2m] = M (mass)
[cos(kx)] = dimensionless (trigonometric function)
[k] = L⁻¹ (given as m⁻¹, where m is meter)
[x] = L (length)
[kx] = L⁻¹ × L = dimensionless

The equation equates L (length) with M (mass), which have different dimensions.

L ≠ M → Dimensionally INCONSISTENT

Final Answers

(a) Dimensionally INCONSISTENT
(b) Dimensionally INCONSISTENT

Note

Dimensional analysis is a powerful tool to check the validity of equations. If an equation is dimensionally inconsistent, it cannot be physically correct. However, dimensional consistency doesn't guarantee the equation is correct - it only ensures the units match.

Problem 6: Vector Addition - Solutions

13 pts

Vector A has a magnitude of 10 units and makes 60° with the positive x-axis. Vector B has a magnitude of 5 units and is directed along the negative x-axis. Find the vector:

Key Concept

Vectors can be added by breaking them into components and then adding the components.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Break vectors into components:

Aₓ = |A|cosθ = 10 cos60° = 10 × 0.5 = 5 units
Aᵧ = |A|sinθ = 10 sin60° = 10 × 0.866 = 8.66 units
Bₓ = |B|cos180° = 5 × (-1) = -5 units
Bᵧ = |B|sin180° = 5 × 0 = 0 units

2 (a) Calculate A + B:

(A + B)ₓ = Aₓ + Bₓ = 5 + (-5) = 0 units
(A + B)ᵧ = Aᵧ + Bᵧ = 8.66 + 0 = 8.66 units

3 Find magnitude and direction of A + B:

|A + B| = √(0² + 8.66²) = √75 = 8.66 units
Direction: tanθ = 8.66/0 (undefined) → θ = 90° (along positive y-axis)

4 (b) Calculate A - B:

(A - B)ₓ = Aₓ - Bₓ = 5 - (-5) = 10 units
(A - B)ᵧ = Aᵧ - Bᵧ = 8.66 - 0 = 8.66 units

5 Find magnitude and direction of A - B:

|A - B| = √(10² + 8.66²) = √(100 + 75) = √175 = 13.23 units
Direction: tanθ = 8.66/10 = 0.866 → θ = tan⁻¹(0.866) = 40.9°

Final Answers

(a) A + B = 8.66 units along positive y-axis
(b) A - B = 13.23 units at 40.9° above positive x-axis

Note

Vector subtraction A - B is equivalent to adding A and -B. The negative of a vector has the same magnitude but opposite direction.

Problem 7: Vector Operations - Solutions

13 pts

Find the cross product of A × B where A = 2i + 3j, B = -i + 2j.

Key Concept

For 2D vectors in the xy-plane, the cross product gives a vector in the z-direction: A × B = (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Identify the components:

Aₓ = 2, Aᵧ = 3, A_z = 0
Bₓ = -1, Bᵧ = 2, B_z = 0

2 Apply the cross product formula:

A × B = (AᵧB_z - A_zBᵧ)i + (A_zBₓ - AₓB_z)j + (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k

3 Calculate each component:

i-component: AᵧB_z - A_zBᵧ = (3)(0) - (0)(2) = 0
j-component: A_zBₓ - AₓB_z = (0)(-1) - (2)(0) = 0
k-component: AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ = (2)(2) - (3)(-1) = 4 - (-3) = 7

4 Write the cross product:

A × B = 0i + 0j + 7k = 7k

5 For 2D vectors, we can use the simplified formula:

A × B = (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k = (2×2 - 3×(-1))k = (4 + 3)k = 7k

Final Answer

A × B = 7k

Note

The cross product of two vectors is always perpendicular to both vectors. For 2D vectors in the xy-plane, the cross product points in the z-direction (either positive or negative). The magnitude of the cross product equals the area of the parallelogram formed by the two vectors.

Problem 8: Physical Applications - Solutions

13 pts

A ship is steaming due east at a speed of 12 m/s. A passenger runs across the deck at a speed of 5 m/s toward north. What is the resultant velocity of the passenger relative to the sea?

Key Concept

The resultant velocity is the vector sum of the ship's velocity and the passenger's velocity relative to the ship.

Step-by-Step Solution

1 Identify the velocities:

Ship's velocity: v_ship = 12 m/s (east)
Passenger's velocity relative to ship: v_passenger/ship = 5 m/s (north)

2 Break velocities into components:

v_ship = 12i + 0j m/s
v_passenger/ship = 0i + 5j m/s

3 Calculate resultant velocity:

v_resultant = v_ship + v_passenger/ship = (12i + 0j) + (0i + 5j) = 12i + 5j m/s

4 Find magnitude of resultant velocity:

|v_resultant| = √(12² + 5²) = √(144 + 25) = √169 = 13 m/s

5 Find direction of resultant velocity:

tanθ = (5/12) = 0.4167
θ = tan⁻¹(0.4167) = 22.6° north of east

Final Answer

Resultant velocity = 13 m/s at 22.6° north of east

Note

This is an example of relative velocity. The passenger's velocity relative to the sea is the vector sum of the ship's velocity relative to the sea and the passenger's velocity relative to the ship. The magnitude follows the Pythagorean theorem since the velocities are perpendicular.
