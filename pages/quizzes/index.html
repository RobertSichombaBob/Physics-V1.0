Question 1: SI Units

10 pts

Which of the following is the SI base unit for mass?

Gram (g)
Kilogram (kg)
Pound (lb)
Newton (N)

Hint

Remember that the kilogram is the only SI base unit that contains a prefix. The gram is a derived unit.

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, the kilogram (kg) is the SI base unit for mass. Although it contains the prefix "kilo", it is defined as the base unit in the International System of Units.

Incorrect

The correct answer is kilogram (kg). While the gram is a metric unit, the kilogram is the SI base unit for mass.

Question 2: Unit Conversion

10 pts

Convert 72 km/h to m/s.

2 m/s
10 m/s
15 m/s
20 m/s

Hint

Remember that 1 km = 1000 m and 1 h = 3600 s. So to convert km/h to m/s, multiply by (1000/3600) or (5/18).

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, 72 km/h = 20 m/s. The calculation is: 72 × (1000/3600) = 72 × (5/18) = 20 m/s.

Incorrect

The correct answer is 20 m/s. To convert km/h to m/s, multiply by (1000/3600) = 5/18. So 72 × (5/18) = 20 m/s.

Question 3: Significant Figures

10 pts

How many significant figures are in the measurement 0.00450 m?

2
4
5
3

Hint

Remember that leading zeros are not significant, but trailing zeros after a decimal point are significant.

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, 0.00450 m has 3 significant figures. The leading zeros are not significant, but the trailing zero after the decimal is significant.

Incorrect

The correct answer is 3. The significant figures are 4, 5, and 0 (the trailing zero after the decimal point). The leading zeros are not significant.

Question 4: Dimensional Analysis

Which of the following equations is dimensionally consistent?

s = ut + ½at²
F = m + a
E = m/v

Hint

Check if all terms in the equation have the same dimensions. Remember that numerical constants like ½ are dimensionless.

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, s = ut + ½at² is dimensionally consistent. All terms have dimensions of length [L].

Incorrect

The correct answer is s = ut + ½at². All terms in this equation have dimensions of length [L]. The other equations add or equate quantities with different dimensions.

Hint

The magnitude of a vector A = xi + yj is given by |A| = √(x² + y²).

Show Hint

Correct!

Incorrect

The correct answer is 5. The magnitude is calculated as |A| = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5.

Question 7: Vector Addition

13 units

Hint

Since the vectors are perpendicular, use the Pythagorean theorem: |A + B| = √(|A|² + |B|²).

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, |A + B| = √(5² + 12²) = √(25 + 144) = √169 = 13 units.

Incorrect

The correct answer is 13 units. Since the vectors are perpendicular, we use the Pythagorean theorem: |A + B| = √(5² + 12²) = √(25 + 144) = √169 = 13.

Question 8: SI Prefixes

10 pts

Which prefix corresponds to 10⁻⁶?

kilo (k)
centi (c)
nano (n)
micro (μ)

Hint

Remember the common SI prefixes: milli (10⁻³), micro (10⁻⁶), nano (10⁻⁹), pico (10⁻¹²).

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, micro (μ) corresponds to 10⁻⁶. For example, 1 micrometer = 10⁻⁶ meters.

Incorrect

The correct answer is micro (μ). The prefix micro corresponds to 10⁻⁶. Nano corresponds to 10⁻⁹, centi to 10⁻², and kilo to 10³.

Question 9: Cross Product

10 pts

For 2D vectors in the xy-plane, the cross product A × B gives a vector in which direction?

x-direction
y-direction
xy-plane
z-direction

Hint

The cross product of two vectors is always perpendicular to both vectors. In 2D, both vectors lie in the xy-plane.

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, for 2D vectors in the xy-plane, A × B gives a vector in the z-direction (either positive or negative).

Incorrect

The correct answer is z-direction. The cross product of two vectors is always perpendicular to both vectors. Since both vectors lie in the xy-plane, their cross product must point in the z-direction.

Question 10: Vector Components

10 pts

A vector has a magnitude of 10 units and makes an angle of 30° with the positive x-axis. What is its y-component?

5 units
7.5 units
8.66 units
5 units

Hint

The y-component of a vector is given by |A|sinθ, where θ is the angle with the positive x-axis.

Show Hint

Correct!

Yes, Aᵧ = |A|sinθ = 10 × sin30° = 10 × 0.5 = 5 units.

Incorrect

The correct answer is 5 units. The y-component is calculated as Aᵧ = |A|sinθ = 10 × sin30° = 10 × 0.5 = 5 units.
