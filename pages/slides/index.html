SI Units and Prefixes

Understanding the International System of Units

SI Base Units

The International System of Units (SI) defines seven base units from which all other units are derived.

Base Units

  • • Length: meter (m)
  • • Time: second (s)
  • • Mass: kilogram (kg)
  • • Temperature: kelvin (K)
  • • Electric current: ampere (A)
  • • Amount of substance: mole (mol)
  • • Luminous intensity: candela (cd)

Common Derived Units

  • • Force: newton (N) = kg·m/s²
  • • Energy: joule (J) = N·m
  • • Power: watt (W) = J/s
  • • Pressure: pascal (Pa) = N/m²

SI Prefixes

SI prefixes are used to form decimal multiples and submultiples of SI units.

Common Prefixes

  • • kilo (k) = 10³
  • • mega (M) = 10⁶
  • • giga (G) = 10⁹
  • • milli (m) = 10⁻³
  • • micro (μ) = 10⁻⁶
  • • nano (n) = 10⁻⁹
  • • pico (p) = 10⁻¹²

Examples

  • • 1 km = 1000 m
  • • 1 mg = 0.001 g
  • • 1 ns = 10⁻⁹ s
  • • 1 GW = 10⁹ W

Unit Conversions

Solving Tutorial Problems with Step-by-Step Solutions

Problem 1: Carpet Installation

A carpet is to be installed in a room whose length is measured to be 13.71 m and whose width is measured to be 4.46 m. Find the area of the room.

Solution

1 Identify the formula for area of a rectangle:

Area = Length × Width

2 Substitute the given values:

Area = 13.71 m × 4.46 m

3 Perform the multiplication:

13.71 × 4.46 = 61.1466 m²

4 Apply significant figures rules:

- Length: 13.71 m (4 significant figures)

- Width: 4.46 m (3 significant figures)

- Result should have 3 significant figures

61.1466 m² → 61.1 m²

5 Final Answer:

Area = 61.1 m²

Problem 2: Volume Conversion

An object in the shape of rectangular parallelepiped measures 2.0 in × 3.5 in × 6.5 in. Determine the volume of the object in m³.

Solution

1 Calculate volume in cubic inches:

Volume = 2.0 in × 3.5 in × 6.5 in = 45.5 in³

2 Convert inches to meters:

1 in = 0.0254 m
1 in³ = (0.0254 m)³ = 1.6387 × 10⁻⁵ m³

3 Convert volume to cubic meters:

Volume = 45.5 in³ × (1.6387 × 10⁻⁵ m³/in³) = 7.456 × 10⁻⁴ m³

4 Apply significant figures:

- All measurements have 2 significant figures

- Result should have 2 significant figures

7.456 × 10⁻⁴ m³ → 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ m³

5 Final Answer:

Volume = 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ m³

Significant Figures and Uncertainty

Precision in Measurements and Calculations

Rules for Significant Figures

Counting Rules

  • • All non-zero digits are significant
  • • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
  • • Leading zeros are not significant
  • • Trailing zeros after a decimal point are significant
  • • Trailing zeros without a decimal point are ambiguous

Calculation Rules

  • • Multiplication/Division: Result has same sig figs as measurement with fewest sig figs
  • • Addition/Subtraction: Result has same decimal places as measurement with fewest decimal places

Problem 6: Uncertainty in Area

A rectangular plate has a length of (21.3 ± 0.2) cm and a width of (9.8 ± 0.1) cm. Calculate the area of the plate including its uncertainty.

Solution

1 Calculate nominal area:

A = 21.3 cm × 9.8 cm = 208.74 cm²

2 Calculate relative uncertainties:

Relative uncertainty in length = 0.2 / 21.3 = 0.00939 (0.939%)
Relative uncertainty in width = 0.1 / 9.8 = 0.01020 (1.020%)

3 For multiplication, relative uncertainties add:

Relative uncertainty in area = 0.00939 + 0.01020 = 0.01959 (1.959%)

4 Calculate absolute uncertainty:

Absolute uncertainty = 208.74 cm² × 0.01959 = 4.087 cm²

5 Round uncertainty to 1 significant figure:

4.087 cm² → 4 cm²

6 Round area to match uncertainty:

208.74 cm² → 209 cm²

7 Final Answer:

Area = 209 ± 4 cm²

Dimensional Analysis

Checking Equations and Deriving Formulas

Fundamental Dimensions

All physical quantities can be expressed in terms of three fundamental dimensions: Length [L], Mass [M], and Time [T].

Common Dimensions

  • • Velocity: [L][T]⁻¹
  • • Acceleration: [L][T]⁻²
  • • Force: [M][L][T]⁻²
  • • Energy: [M][L]²[T]⁻²
  • • Pressure: [M][L]⁻¹[T]⁻²

Dimensional Consistency

For an equation to be physically valid, all terms must have the same dimensions.

v = u + at

[L][T]⁻¹ = [L][T]⁻¹ + [L][T]⁻² × [T] = [L][T]⁻¹ + [L][T]⁻¹

✓ Dimensionally consistent

Problem 15: Dimensional Analysis

Analyze the following equations to determine whether dimensionally correct or not:

(a) v = u + ax

(b) y = (2m)cos(kx) where k = 2m⁻¹

Solution (a)

1 Identify dimensions:

[v] = [u] = [L][T]⁻¹ (velocity)
[a] = [L][T]⁻² (acceleration)
[x] = [L] (distance)
[ax] = [L][T]⁻² × [L] = [L]²[T]⁻²

2 Check consistency:

[L][T]⁻¹ ≠ [L]²[T]⁻²

The equation adds quantities with different dimensions.

3 Conclusion:

Equation (a) is dimensionally INCONSISTENT

Solution (b)

1 Identify dimensions:

[y] = [L] (length)
[2m] = [M] (mass)
[cos(kx)] = dimensionless
[k] = [L]⁻¹ (given as m⁻¹)
[x] = [L] (length)
[kx] = [L]⁻¹ × [L] = dimensionless ✓

2 Check consistency:

[L] ≠ [M]

The equation equates length with mass.

3 Conclusion:

Equation (b) is dimensionally INCONSISTENT

Introduction to Vectors

Quantities with Magnitude and Direction

Scalars vs. Vectors

Scalar Quantities

Have magnitude only

  • • Mass
  • • Temperature
  • • Time
  • • Distance
  • • Speed
  • • Energy

Vector Quantities

Have both magnitude and direction

  • • Displacement
  • • Velocity
  • • Acceleration
  • • Force
  • • Momentum
  • • Electric field

Vector Representation

Component Form

A = Aₓi + Aᵧj

Where i and j are unit vectors in the x and y directions.

Magnitude and Direction

|A| = √(Aₓ² + Aᵧ²)
θ = tan⁻¹(Aᵧ/Aₓ)

Graphical Representation

Vector A with components Aₓ and Aᵧ

Vector Addition

Graphical Method

Place vectors head to tail. The resultant is from the tail of the first to the head of the last.

Component Method

Rₓ = Aₓ + Bₓ
Rᵧ = Aᵧ + Bᵧ
R = √(Rₓ² + Rᵧ²)
θ = tan⁻¹(Rᵧ/Rₓ)

Visualization

Vector A + B = R (resultant)

Vector Operations

Addition, Subtraction, Dot Product, and Cross Product

Problem 1: Vector Addition and Subtraction

Vector A has a magnitude of 10 units and makes 60° with the positive x-axis. Vector B has a magnitude of 5 units and is directed along the negative x-axis. Find:

(i) A + B

(ii) A - B

Solution

1 Break vectors into components:

Aₓ = |A|cosθ = 10 cos60° = 10 × 0.5 = 5 units
Aᵧ = |A|sinθ = 10 sin60° = 10 × 0.866 = 8.66 units
Bₓ = |B|cos180° = 5 × (-1) = -5 units
Bᵧ = |B|sin180° = 5 × 0 = 0 units

2 (i) Calculate A + B:

(A + B)ₓ = Aₓ + Bₓ = 5 + (-5) = 0 units
(A + B)ᵧ = Aᵧ + Bᵧ = 8.66 + 0 = 8.66 units

3 Find magnitude and direction of A + B:

|A + B| = √(0² + 8.66²) = √75 = 8.66 units
Direction: tanθ = 8.66/0 (undefined) → θ = 90°

4 (ii) Calculate A - B:

(A - B)ₓ = Aₓ - Bₓ = 5 - (-5) = 10 units
(A - B)ᵧ = Aᵧ - Bᵧ = 8.66 - 0 = 8.66 units

5 Find magnitude and direction of A - B:

|A - B| = √(10² + 8.66²) = √(100 + 75) = √175 = 13.23 units
Direction: tanθ = 8.66/10 = 0.866 → θ = 40.9°

6 Final Answers:

(i) A + B = 8.66 units along positive y-axis
(ii) A - B = 13.23 units at 40.9° above positive x-axis

Dot and Cross Products

Vector Multiplication Operations

Dot Product (Scalar Product)

Definition

A · B = |A||B|cosθ

Where θ is the angle between vectors A and B.

Component Form

A · B = AₓBₓ + AᵧBᵧ + A₂B₂

Properties

  • • Commutative: A · B = B · A
  • • Distributive: A · (B + C) = A · B + A · C
  • • Result is a scalar

Cross Product (Vector Product)

A × B = |A||B|sinθ n

Where n is a unit vector perpendicular to both A and B.

Component Form (3D)

A × B = (AᵧB₂ - A₂Bᵧ)i + (A₂Bₓ - AₓB₂)j + (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k

Properties

  • • Anti-commutative: A × B = -B × A
  • • Distributive: A × (B + C) = A × B + A × C
  • • Result is a vector

Problem 9: Cross Product

Find the cross product of A × B where A = 2i + 3j, B = -i + 2j.

Solution

1 Identify components:

Aₓ = 2, Aᵧ = 3, A₂ = 0
Bₓ = -1, Bᵧ = 2, B₂ = 0

2 Apply cross product formula:

A × B = (AᵧB₂ - A₂Bᵧ)i + (A₂Bₓ - AₓB₂)j + (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k

3 Calculate each component:

i-component: AᵧB₂ - A₂Bᵧ = (3)(0) - (0)(2) = 0
j-component: A₂Bₓ - AₓB₂ = (0)(-1) - (2)(0) = 0
k-component: AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ = (2)(2) - (3)(-1) = 4 - (-3) = 7

4 Write the cross product:

A × B = 0i + 0j + 7k = 7k

5 For 2D vectors, simplified formula:

A × B = (AₓBᵧ - AᵧBₓ)k = (2×2 - 3×(-1))k = (4 + 3)k = 7k

6 Final Answer:

A × B = 7k

Vector Applications

Solving Real-World Physics Problems

Problem 4: Relative Velocity

A ship is steaming due east at a speed of 12 m/s. A passenger runs across the deck at a speed of 5 m/s toward north. What is the resultant velocity of the passenger relative to the sea?

Solution

1 Identify velocities:

v_ship = 12 m/s (east)
v_passenger/ship = 5 m/s (north)

2 Break into components:

v_ship = 12i + 0j m/s
v_passenger/ship = 0i + 5j m/s

3 Calculate resultant velocity:

v_resultant = v_ship + v_passenger/ship = (12i + 0j) + (0i + 5j) = 12i + 5j m/s

4 Find magnitude:

|v_resultant| = √(12² + 5²) = √(144 + 25) = √169 = 13 m/s

5 Find direction:

tanθ = 5/12 = 0.4167
θ = tan⁻¹(0.4167) = 22.6° north of east

6 Final Answer:

Resultant velocity = 13 m/s at 22.6° north of east

Problem 3: Treasure Hunt

Three contestants are given three displacements: 72.4 m, 32.0° east of north; 57.3 m, 36.0° south of west; 17.8 m straight south. The displacements lead to a point where keys are buried. Calculate the resultant displacement.

Solution Strategy

1 Convert each displacement to component form:

D1: 72.4 m, 32.0° east of north
D2: 57.3 m, 36.0° south of west
D3: 17.8 m straight south

2 Calculate components:

D1ₓ = 72.4 sin32.0° = 72.4 × 0.5299 = 38.4 m (east)
D1ᵧ = 72.4 cos32.0° = 72.4 × 0.8480 = 61.4 m (north)
D2ₓ = 57.3 cos36.0° = 57.3 × 0.8090 = 46.4 m (west, so negative)
D2ᵧ = 57.3 sin36.0° = 57.3 × 0.5878 = 33.7 m (south, so negative)
D3ₓ = 0 m
D3ᵧ = -17.8 m (south)

3 Sum components:

Rₓ = 38.4 - 46.4 + 0 = -8.0 m (west)
Rᵧ = 61.4 - 33.7 - 17.8 = 9.9 m (north)

4 Find magnitude and direction:

|R| = √((-8.0)² + (9.9)²) = √(64 + 98.0) = √162 = 12.7 m
θ = tan⁻¹(9.9/8.0) = tan⁻¹(1.2375) = 51.0° north of west

5 Final Answer:

Resultant displacement = 12.7 m at 51.0° north of west
